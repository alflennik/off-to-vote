Why is this important?

The shrinking of polling volunteers fearing COVID-19, combined with the closing of polling stations, has led to huge lines of people waiting to vote in recent elections. It is not ok. We believe no one should have to give up on their voting rights because of their job. We believe employers should support their employees’ voting rights and give them the flexibility to stand in line to cast their ballot. We believe every vote counts.

Isn’t this already a law?

Giving employees time off to cast a ballot is not a federal obligation for employers. Some states, including Tennessee and Alaska, mandate employers to give employees (paid) time to vote. Others, including Connecticut and Idaho, have zeo voter-leave laws. Some, like Oklahoma and New York, have exceptions that can make it difficult for employees to stand up for their rights. Confusing, right? Here’s a 2018 article that details the laws, state by state.

Shouldn’t Election Day be a Saturday? Or a National Holiday?

We hear you. But it’s not going to happen before November 3, 2020. So in the meantime, thanks for considering taking the offtovote.org pledge.

For individuals

I am considering taking the pledge as an individual. What does that entail?

If you pledge as an individual, we ask that you dedicate 4 hours of your day on November 3, 2020, to make sure you have enough time to cast your ballot. It shouldn’t have to take that long and we hope it won’t.

I took the pledge as an individual. Now what?

  1. Ask your employer to step up its game. By now, you should have received a confirmation email from us. Go ahead and forward it to your manager and ask if your company will consider taking the pledge on behalf of all your colleagues.
  2. Email your friends, post on your social media account(s) and shout it from the rooftops: we need more eligible voters to follow your lead.
  3. Thank you for helping us boost voter turnout. See you at the polling station!

For employers and team leads

As an employer, I am considering taking the pledge. What does that entail?

We ask that you give all your employees the option to take 4 hours of paid time off on November 3, 2020. We also ask that you don’t deduce these 4 hours from their PTO or any other leave your company already offers. We ask that you don’t ask for proof of voting, as some of your employees may want to use this time to help others get to the polls safely. It’s the right thing to do.

I took the pledge as an employer. Now what?

  1. Tell your employees what you’ve committed to so they know they have your support in casting their ballot. To make this as smooth as possible, we recommend sharing the news with your employees by October 27, 2020 (the Tuesday prior to Election Day) so your team can get organized, whether that means staggering shifts or “out of office” emails.
  2. Email your friends, post on your company’s social media and shout it from the rooftops: we need more companies to follow your lead.
  3. Thank you for helping us boost voter turnout. See you at the polling station!
