About

Voting is the action verb of democracy. The right to cast a ballot was the dream of our founders. Generations of civil rights activists have fought and bled for it. And the brave men and women of our military put their lives on the line every day to defend it.

The vast majority of Americans agree that high turnout is important, especially in presidential elections. But in 2016, only 56% of the voting age public cast ballots. That puts America 26th out of 32 highly developed democracies —behind Mexico, the Czech Republic, and Estonia.

In 2014, two thirds of the registered voters who stayed home said that they didn’t have time to vote. 35% of them chalked it up to work or school conflicts. That's millions of voters who could not get time off to do their civic duty.

We can do better. This year, give your team the chance to make a difference. Give them the day Off to Vote.
